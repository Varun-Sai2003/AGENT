T2 ARMOR MASS PRODUCTION — CHEAT SHEET (Level 20–50)
======================================================

Zone: Oak Forest (T2-3). T2 armor uses Jute (cloth), Thin Leather,
and Bronze Ingots. It covers the level 20-50 range and is REQUIRED
before entering any T3 zone (Canyon / Old Mine).


======================================================
 PRE-FLIGHT CHECKLIST
======================================================

  Requirement                  | How to Check
  -----------------------------|---------------------------------------------------
  Armor Workshop Lv.2          | Tap Workshop > Upgrade > T2 recipes must show
  Foundry Lv.2                 | Required for Bronze Ingots (metal armor parts)
  Tanner bench (ranch)         | Needed to craft Thin Leather from Animal Hides
  Jute Blueprint               | Unlocked via Armor Workshop Lv.2 by default
  Thin Leather Blueprint       | Tanner bench menu — confirm it's available
  Wagon + 3-4 spare axes       | Jute requires chopping in Oak Forest
  Storage chests cleared       | Jute + Hide + Ore take up lots of space

  WARNING: Floor tiles under Armor Workshop AND Foundry AND Tanner
  must match the building tier or the Upgrade button stays greyed out.
  Upgrade ALL bench floors first.

  UPGRADE ORDER (if starting fresh):
    1. Foundry Lv.2  (Bronze Ingots unlock)
    2. Tanner Lv.2   (Thin Leather unlock)
    3. Armor Workshop Lv.2  (T2 armor recipes unlock)


======================================================
 FULL MATERIAL CHAIN — T2 ARMOR SET
======================================================

  NOTE: Quantities below are baseline (Blueprint Lv.1) estimates based
  on community data. Exact amounts scale with blueprint level.
  To verify YOUR numbers: Armor Workshop > tap any piece > see recipe.

  PROCESSING RATIOS:
    3x Jute Plant  = 1x Jute Cloth  (Tool Bench)
    1-2x Thin Hide = 1x Leather     (Tanner Lv.2)

  ── PER PIECE BREAKDOWN (Processed Materials) ──────────────────────

  Piece            | Jute Cloth | Leather | Notes
  -----------------|------------|---------|----------------------------
  Hat (Leather Hat)| 3          | 2       | Cheapest piece — farm first
  Jacket           | 5          | 4       | Most expensive — plan extra
  Jeans (Pants)    | 4          | 3       | Mid-cost
  Boots            | 3          | 3       | Same cost range as hat
  Gloves           | 2          | 2       | Smallest — sometimes skipped

  ── FULL SET TOTALS ────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Processed Material  | Full Set (5 pieces) | Raw Equivalent
  --------------------|---------------------|------------------------
  Jute Cloth          | ~17                 | ~51 Jute Plants
  Leather             | ~14                 | ~14-28 Thin Hides

  ── RAW FARMING TARGET (for 1 full set) ────────────────────────────

  Resource       | Amount to Farm | Where
  ---------------|----------------|----------------------------------
  Jute Plants    | ~50-55         | Oak Forest — plant/herb nodes
  Thin Hides     | ~20-30         | Wolves + Boars (Oak Forest)
  Copper Ore     | ~10-15         | Copper Mine (T1 zone)
  Tin Ore        | ~10-15         | Tin Mine (T2 zone)
  Brown Coal     | ~5-8           | Any mine (spawns with ore)

  TIP: Hides and Jute are your bottleneck — not ore. Prioritize
  hunting boars and wolves in Oak Forest on every farming trip.
  Jacket costs the most — stockpile extra Jute Cloth before crafting.


======================================================
 MATERIAL PROCESSING CHAIN
======================================================

  RAW > PROCESSED > CRAFTED

  Animal Hide  ──> [Tanner Lv.2]  ──> Thin Leather
  Jute Plant   ──> [Tool Bench]   ──> Jute Cloth / Jute Rope
  Copper + Tin + Coal ──> [Foundry Lv.2] ──> Bronze Ingot
  All above    ──> [Armor Workshop Lv.2] ──> T2 Armor Piece

  QUEUE ORDER (same principle as gun production):
    1st > Foundry        (smelt Bronze Ingots — slowest)
    2nd > Tanner         (process Hides > Thin Leather)
    3rd > Tool Bench     (craft Jute Cloth / Rope)
    4th > Armor Workshop (assemble final armor — queue last)


======================================================
 FARMING ROUTES (Energy-Optimized)
======================================================

  Route A — Full Material Run (Best for bulk sets)
  -------------------------------------------------
  Ranch
    > Oak Forest   (priority: hunt wolves + boars > Hides)
                   (secondary: collect Jute plant nodes)
                   (also: kill bandits for XP + loot)
    > Copper Mine  (grab Copper Ore + Brown Coal)
    > Tin Mine     (grab Tin Ore)
    > Ranch
    > Queue: Foundry > Tanner > Tool Bench > sleep overnight

  Route B — Hide-Focused Run (Thin Leather bottleneck fix)
  ---------------------------------------------------------
  Ranch
    > Oak Forest   (hunt ONLY: wolves, boars, bears)
                   (ignore plants and ore — full inventory for hides)
    > Ranch        (process all hides at Tanner immediately)
    > Repeat next session for Jute/Ore

  Route C — Trade Shortcut (Busy or low-energy day)
  --------------------------------------------------
  Silverton
    > Tanner merchant   (buy Thin Leather directly if well-stocked)
    > Weaver merchant   (buy Jute Cloth / Rope)
    > Metals Merchant   (5 Copper > 1 Bronze Ingot equivalent)
  Ranch
    > Skip farming entirely > craft armor from purchased mats

  TIP: The Tanner and Weaver merchants in Silverton reset every 24h.
  On low-energy days, a single Silverton visit can supply a
  full armor set's worth of processed materials — no hunting needed.


======================================================
 DAILY PRODUCTION LOOP
======================================================

  -- LOGIN --------------------------------------------------------
  1. Start Foundry: queue all Copper + Tin + Coal > Bronze Ingots
  2. Start Tanner: queue all Animal Hides > Thin Leather

  -- WHILE BENCHES RUN -------------------------------------------
  3. Use full energy on Route A (Oak Forest > Mines > Ranch)
  4. Return > add new Hides to Tanner, new Ore to Foundry

  -- BEFORE SLEEP ------------------------------------------------
  5. Craft Jute Cloth and Jute Rope at Tool Bench
  6. Queue Armor Workshop with as many pieces as mats allow
  7. Log out > armor crafts passively overnight

  -- NEXT LOGIN --------------------------------------------------
  8. Collect finished armor > repair bench check > repeat step 1

  OVERNIGHT QUEUE PRIORITY:
    1st > Foundry         (longest queue — Bronze Ingots)
    2nd > Tanner          (Thin Leather processing)
    3rd > Tool Bench      (Jute Cloth / Rope — fast)
    4th > Armor Workshop  (queue last before sleep)

======================================================
 T2 ARMOR RARITY SYSTEM — ALL GRADES EXPLAINED
======================================================

  IMPORTANT: All rarity grades at T2 use the SAME raw materials
  (Jute Cloth + Leather). Rarity is determined by the BLUEPRINT —
  not the crafting recipe. Better blueprint = better stats crafted.

  ── RARITY GRADES ──────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Grade      | How to Get Blueprint         | Stat Benefit
  -----------|------------------------------|-----------------------------------
  Common     | Default Armor Workshop Lv.2  | Base defense — enough for Oak Forest
  Uncommon   | Gustav trade (Common>Uncommon)| +10-15% defense vs. Common
  Rare       | Rare Tubes — Bandit Camps,   | Bonus stat (e.g. +Spirit, +Attack
             | Smuggler Adam McCoy          | Speed, +defense vs. specific enemy)
  Epic       | Season Pass / Industrial     | Highest T2 stats + strong secondary
             | Tubes (rare drop)            | bonus — best-in-tier performance

  ── BLUEPRINT LEVELING (v9.4+ update) ──────────────────────────────

  Finding the SAME blueprint again lets you UPGRADE its level at the
  Armor Workshop. Higher blueprint level = higher stats on each craft.

  - T1 and T2 blueprint upgrades cost SILVER ONLY (no rare mats needed)
  - Each upgrade level improves: Defense, Durability, and bonus stat values
  - Max blueprint level gives the highest stats possible for that rarity

  HOW TO LEVEL UP:
    1. Find a duplicate blueprint tube (same item, same rarity)
    2. Go to Armor Workshop > select item > tap Upgrade Blueprint
    3. Pay the Silver cost > blueprint levels up permanently

  ── STAT DIFFERENCES BY RARITY (T2 approximate) ────────────────────

  Rarity    | Defense  | Durability | Bonus Stat
  ----------|----------|------------|------------------------------------------
  Common    | Base     | Base       | None
  Uncommon  | +10-15%  | +10%       | None or minor
  Rare      | +20-30%  | +15-20%    | 1 active bonus (Spirit/Speed/enemy resist)
  Epic      | +40-50%  | +25-30%    | 1-2 strong bonuses + higher resist values

  ── BONUS STAT TYPES ON RARE/EPIC T2 ARMOR ─────────────────────────

  Bonus Type           | Effect                           | Best For
  ---------------------|----------------------------------|--------------------
  +Spirit              | Reduces animal aggro radius      | Wrangler / farming
  +Attack Speed        | Faster combat actions            | Bandit farming / XP
  +Defense vs. Bandits | Less damage from humanoid enemies| Oak Forest runs
  +Defense vs. Animals | Less damage from wildlife        | Hide/Jute farming
  +Cold Resistance     | Survive colder zones longer      | Canyon (T3 prep)
  +Heat Resistance     | Survive hot zones (T4 prep)      | Future Cotton Plains

  ── V11.0 ARMOR MECHANICS UPDATE (December 2025) ────────────────────

  - Firearm resistance bonuses now apply PER BODY PART the armor covers
    (e.g., a chest piece with gun resist only protects your torso)
  - Each armor piece defense value is DOUBLED vs. pre-v11 to compensate
  - No traditional "set bonuses" — each piece contributes independently
  - RESULT: Mixing Rare/Epic pieces with Common is now viable —
    prioritize the highest-rarity piece for the body slot taking most hits

  ── RARITY PRIORITY UNLOCK ORDER ────────────────────────────────────

  1. Common set (Armor Workshop Lv.2 default) — get this on Day 1
  2. Uncommon via Gustav daily trades — target jacket first (most wear)
  3. Rare jacket + hat from Bandit Camps — highest defense priority
  4. Rare gloves with +Spirit or +Attack Speed — quality-of-life boost
  5. Epic pieces only if Season Pass active — don't grind for these


  NOTE: Don't invest in Epic T2 until all Rare T2 slots are filled.
  The time-cost to farm Epic T2 is better spent transitioning to T3.

======================================================
 SELL vs. KEEP DECISIONS
======================================================

  Situation                      | Action
  -------------------------------|------------------------------------------------
  Full set crafted, have surplus | Sell extras at Silverton > Silver Coins
  Armor at <30% durability       | Repair Bench — always cheaper than re-crafting
  Rare T2 variant dropped        | Keep — Rare perks are worth more than Silver
  Season Pass active             | Check "craft X armor" tasks > Golden Bullets
  In an Alliance                 | Donate surplus T2 armor > Alliance Points
  No Jute — have Hides           | Use Silverton Weaver to fill Jute gap same day
  No Hides — have Jute           | Hunt one session in Oak Forest (boars priority)

  DURABILITY RULE:
  - Repair at ~35% remaining (not 0%)
  - Once armor hits 0% it is DESTROYED — cannot be recovered
  - Buffalo Fish (cooked) can repair gear — more efficient than Silver


======================================================
 TOP GOTCHAS
======================================================

  Mistake                        | Consequence                 | Fix
  -------------------------------|-----------------------------|--------------------------
  Tanner below Lv.2             | Can't make Thin Leather     | Upgrade BEFORE stockpile
  Ignoring Jute nodes in forest  | Hide surplus, no cloth      | Collect Jute every Oak run
  Armor hitting 0% durability    | Piece permanently destroyed | Repair at 35%, not 0%
  Buying Thin Leather (wasting)  | Silver drain for free item  | Hunt boars/wolves — free
  Wrong zone for T2 armor use    | Fast durability loss        | Match armor tier to zone
  Entering T3 zone in T2 armor   | Accelerated destruction     | Only use T2 in T2 zones
  Crafting full set all at once  | Jute/Hide bottleneck stalls | Craft piece by piece daily
  Selling Hides at Fur Trader    | Lost Thin Leather supply    | Process Hides > sell excess

  CRITICAL RULE: Never enter Canyon (T3) or higher in T2 armor.
  Damage taken in a higher-tier zone destroys gear exponentially faster.
  Get a full T3 set BEFORE progressing past Oak Forest.

======================================================
 T2 ARMOR vs T2 FIREARMS — RESOURCE OVERLAP
======================================================

  If you are mass producing BOTH T2 guns AND T2 armor simultaneously:

  SHARED resources (compete for same stock):
    > Bronze Ingots   (guns AND armor both need them)
    > Foundry queue   (single bottleneck for both)

  SEPARATE resources (no conflict):
    > Guns:   Copper + Tin + Coal (ore-focused)
    > Armor:  Jute + Animal Hides (forest-focused)

  STRATEGY: Do one ore run (Route A from gun guide) + one Oak Forest
  hunt in the same session. Smelt ore overnight in Foundry, process
  hides at Tanner simultaneously. Both production lines run in parallel.


======================================================
 TL;DR — THE 3-STEP T2 ARMOR FORMULA
======================================================

  1. HUNT    > Oak Forest daily: wolves + boars > Hides > Thin Leather
  2. QUEUE   > Foundry (Bronze) + Tanner (Leather) overnight in parallel
  3. CRAFT   > Jute Cloth at Tool Bench > Armor Workshop before sleep

  Full T2 set in 2-3 days. Repair, don't replace. Never hit 0% durability.
